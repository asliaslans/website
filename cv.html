Research Program

Dr. Aslan's research addresses the sources, persistence, and mitigation of environmental hazards in water environments. Her work sits at the intersection of public health, environmental sciences, and engineering, with outcomes aimed at supporting rapid detection of environmental pollutants, science-driven decision-making during emergencies, environmental stewardship within communities, and workforce development in water resource management and public health.

  • 40+ peer-reviewed publications in high-impact journals.
  • 1,100+ citations
  • Research focus areas: water quality, environmental health, rapid diagnostics, community-based research, and applied water systems innovation.
  • Innovation in portable sensing technologies for water quality monitoring.
  • Across a research career spanning 2001–present, secured $8.5M in grant and contract funding across 21 awards, serving as Principal Investigator or Co-Principal Investigator on 19 of them.
